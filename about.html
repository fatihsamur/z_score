About Our Z-Score Calculator

A z-score, also known as a standard score, is a measure of how many standard deviations a particular value is from the mean of a dataset. Z-scores are often used in statistics to identify how unusual a particular observation is. For example, a z-score of 2.0 would indicate that the value is two standard deviations above the mean, which is considered to be quite unusual.

To calculate a z-score, you need to know the mean and standard deviation of the dataset, as well as the value you want to evaluate. The formula for calculating a z-score is:

z-score = (value - mean) / standard deviation

Z-scores are often used in a variety of applications, including:

  • Quality control: In manufacturing and other industries, z-scores can be used to identify whether a particular product or process is operating within acceptable limits.
  • Medical diagnosis: In medicine, z-scores can be used to identify whether a particular measurement (such as height or weight) is within the normal range for a particular age and gender.
  • Education: In education, z-scores can be used to compare the performance of students in a particular class to the performance of students in other classes or to a national average.
  • Finance: In finance, z-scores can be used to assess the likelihood of a company defaulting on its debts.
